Challenging climate change

It is clear from this breakdown that a range of sectors and processes contribute to global emissions. This means there is no single or simple solution to tackle climate change. Focusing on electricity, or transport, or food, or deforestation alone is insufficient.

To reach net-zero emissions we need innovations across many sectors. Single solutions will not get us there.

So.. how can the average person do their part?





Addressing Energy Production

Greenhouse Gas Emissions Reduction: Renewable energy sources produce electricity without emitting significant greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4). Fossil fuels, on the other hand, release substantial amounts of CO2 when burned for energy. By transitioning from fossil fuels to renewables, we can reduce these emissions, which are the primary drivers of global warming and climate change. Diet and Mindful food consumption

You can actually help reduce your emissions in a simple way, on a day to day basis. Diets containing more animal-based foods have higher environmental impacts. Per unit of food consumed, meat and dairy has anywhere from three to 100 times the environmental impact of plant-based foods.

It is crucial to note that nuts can actually decrease atmospheric CO2 levels. Please reflect on this data and acknowledge how making healthier choices can benefit not only you but also our planet.
